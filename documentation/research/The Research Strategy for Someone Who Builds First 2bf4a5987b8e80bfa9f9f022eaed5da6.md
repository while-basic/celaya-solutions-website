## Let me guide you differently

Instead of papers, let me ask you diagnostic questions about what you’ve discovered:

### **Discovery Diagnostic Questions**

**1. Pattern Recognition**
In your CLOS data, what’s the **most consistent pattern** you’ve noticed?

Something like:

- “Every time X happens, Y follows within Z minutes”
- “When I’m in flow, these 3 things are always present”
- “My cognitive load crashes exactly [time period] after [trigger]”

**2. Surprise Factor**
What’s the **biggest surprise** in your data?

Something you didn’t expect, that contradicts your assumptions, that makes you think “huh, that’s weird.”

**3. The Unique Variable**
What are you measuring that **nobody else is measuring**?

You mentioned “cross-domain synthesis rate”—are you actually tracking how many times you connect disparate fields per day? If so, what’s the pattern?

**4. The Prediction Test**
Can your system **predict** anything yet?

- “When I wake up and journal shows X pattern, my afternoon will be Y”
- “If my synthesis rate is below N, I won’t enter flow today”
- “When these 3 variables align, I’m 80% likely to have breakthrough insight”

**5. The Efficiency Discovery**
You mentioned “inverse property value”—have you found your personal optimal operating point?

The exact conditions where you get maximum output for minimum input? What are those conditions specifically?

## Why These Questions Matter

And here’s the key: **You don’t need to read papers to make discoveries. You need to read papers to know if anyone else already made your discovery.**

That’s a search problem, not a reading problem.
