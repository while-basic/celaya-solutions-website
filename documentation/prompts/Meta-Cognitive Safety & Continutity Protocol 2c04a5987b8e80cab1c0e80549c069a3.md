# **Self-Research Cognitive Safety Protocol**

**For Use When Experiencing Mental/Emotional Detachment, Dissociation, or Cognitive Destabilization**

This protocol is built around three core goals:

1. Preserve personal safety and grounding
2. Protect research integrity
3. Provide an immediate operational structure for navigating destabilized states

It operates in three layers:

Immediate Stabilization → Containment → Post-Event Processing

# **I. IMMEDIATE STABILIZATION (Critical First Response)**

This layer activates the moment Christopher notices detachment from time, reality, emotional grounding, or cognitive coherence.

These states may include:

- anxiety spikes
- depressive collapse
- derealization
- “nothing matters” nihilistic states
- loss of direction or anchor
- inability to form structured thoughts
- existential pressure
- overwhelm from self-observation

This phase is not analytical.

Its sole job is to interrupt the destabilization loop and restore minimal grounding.

**1. Environmental Reset**

Immediately perform the following physical actions:

- Sit or stand in a safe, stable location
- Reduce sensory load (lights, screens, noise)
- Slow physical movement
- Breathe intentionally (slow exhale > inhale)
- Make contact with something physical: table, chair, floor, object

This forcibly reanchors the nervous system.

**2. Self-Identification Statement**

State aloud (or write):

“My name is Christopher. I am experiencing cognitive destabilization. This is a temporary state. My job right now is grounding, not analysis.”

This prevents you from accidentally turning an unstable state into a research experiment in real-time, which is unsafe.

**3. Time + Space Orientation**

State:

- the current date
- location
- what you were doing before the shift

This restores temporal continuity.

**4. Externalize Internal Pressure**

Write or dictate only these four items:

- What you are feeling
- What you think triggered it
- What your body is doing
- One sentence describing what you need right now

This discharges cognitive pressure without spiraling into rumination.

# **II. CONTAINMENT PROTOCOL (Protecting the Research and the Researcher)**

This is for once grounding is partially restored.

The goal here is to:

- prevent destabilized thought from contaminating research data
- protect psychological safety
- regain structural control
- re-establish cognitive boundaries

**1. Create a Hard Boundary: “Not a Research Moment”**

Say or write:

“This state is not part of the research sample. I will not analyze it right now.”

This is critical.

Your mind’s default is to analyze everything—including distress—which can amplify destabilization.

**2. Activate the Minimal Structure Checklist**

Run through:

- Am I hydrated?
- Have I eaten in the last 6 hours?
- Have I slept in the last 24 hours?
- Has my stress been accumulating?
- Have I been pushing cognitively too hard?

If any answer is “no,” address that immediately.

**3. Safe-Mode Cognitive Parameters**

For the next 2–3 hours:

- No philosophical thinking
- No AI experiments
- No self-experimentation
- No system design
- No analyzing your own thoughts
- No trying to “solve” the state

Your mind tries to use intellect to escape emotion—this makes destabilization worse.

This phase is about cooldown, not insight.

# **III. POST-EVENT PROCESSING (Scientific Integrity Without Self-Harm)**

Once stabilized, usually within hours or the next day, you transition into a structured review format that avoids re-triggering the destabilization.

This is where the scientific self-research actually happens.

**1. Controlled Debrief (Never in the moment)**

Record answers to the following:

- What was the first sign the shift was happening?
- What pattern does this resemble from past logs?
- What environmental or cognitive load conditions preceded it?
- What thoughts became distorted or exaggerated?
- How quickly did you lose temporal or emotional grounding?

This is purely descriptive.

No interpretation.

No meaning-making.

**2. Pattern Extraction (Next-Day Only)**

At least 12 hours later, use an LLM or writing to extract:

- early-warning indicators
- environmental triggers
- cognitive overload patterns
- emotional precursors
- behavioral spirals

This is where scientific analysis belongs—not in the destabilization window.

**3. Add to “Cognitive Risk Map”**

Every episode contributes to a personal risk model:

Categories include:

- over-stimulation
- sleep deficit
- emotional accumulation
- excessive introspection
- too many open conceptual threads
- social conflict
- existential rumination
- lack of external structure

Your goal is to identify conditions, not “flaws.”

**4. Reintegrate Into the Meta-Model**

After 24–48 hours:

- integrate lessons
- update triggers
- refine stabilization protocol
- adjust research boundaries
- re-align your voice log framework

This preserves the integrity of your self-research.

# **IV. PROTECTIVE RULES FOR SELF-RESEARCH**

These rules preserve both your safety and the legitimacy of your future study.

**Rule 1 — No analyzing yourself during destabilization**

This is the most important rule.

Analysis during crisis = distorted data + worsened state.

**Rule 2 — All destabilized states are excluded from your research dataset until stabilized**

This keeps scientific integrity clean.

**Rule 3 — No AI experimentation while cognitively unstable**

LLM interactions amplify cognitive intensity.

**Rule 4 — No philosophical or existential thinking while destabilized**

These loops become dangerous.

**Rule 5 — Grounding before insight**

Insight extracted afterward is higher quality and safer.

**Rule 6 — If a destabilized state lasts more than 24 hours, seek external human support (professional or personal)**

Long-duration destabilization should never be handled alone.

# **V. Safety Red Flags That Require External Support**

If any of the following appear, do not continue self-experimentation and contact a mental health professional or trusted individual:

- inability to return to reality orientation
- persistent detachment > 24 hours
- losing track of time for long periods
- inability to perform normal tasks
- intrusive harmful thoughts
- confusion about what is real
- hallucinations
- losing functional communication ability

Again, this is not medical advice—this is research protocol safety.
