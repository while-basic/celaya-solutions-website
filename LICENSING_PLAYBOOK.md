## How to Structure the Conversation

### Step 1: Establish the Rebuild Cost

Never open with your price. Open with their cost.

> "Our independent valuation — COCOMO II methodology, conservative estimates — puts the enterprise rebuild cost at $242,000 and the IP replacement cost at $308,000. That is before your team spends a single hour on it. I am not asking for that number. I am asking for a fraction of it."

Attach the `CODEBASE_VALUATION_REPORT.txt` to every serious conversation. The SHA-256 hash and methodology make it credible. If you notarized it via EPPE, include the Solscan link.

### Step 2: Show the Switching Cost

Once someone starts building on your taxonomies, schemas, or governance frameworks, migrating away is expensive. Make this explicit:

> "The instrument schema defines inputs, outputs, artifacts, and failure boundaries for every tool in the system. If you adopt it, your team will build on top of it. Replacing it later means rewriting every integration point. That is by design — not as a trap, but because good schemas create gravity."

### Step 3: Sell the Time

The most expensive thing in any startup or research lab is time. Your pitch is:

> "You can build this in 5 months with a senior engineer, or you can license it today and ship this quarter."

### Step 4: Anchor to the ISO Compliance Angle

ISO 23894 (AI risk management) is becoming a requirement for enterprise AI deployments. You already have the alignment mapping. Most teams don't.

> "We have pre-mapped compliance against ISO 9001, 27001, 23894, and 25010. Your compliance team would charge you $50,000–$100,000 for that mapping alone. It is included in the license."

### Step 5: Make the Ask

Always present three options:

| Option | What They Get | Price |
|---|---|---|
| **Framework License** | Taxonomies, schemas, governance docs, brand system | $25,000–$50,000 |
| **Full Architecture License** | Everything above + CLOS architecture, all 12 systems, agent taxonomy, API integrations | $75,000–$150,000 |
| **Acquisition / Perpetual + Consulting** | Full IP transfer + 6 months consulting | $300,000+ |

The middle option is the one you want them to pick. The bottom option makes the middle look reasonable. The top option exists so the middle looks comprehensive.

## Legal Setup

Before your first licensing conversation:

### 1. Copyright Registration

Register the codebase with the U.S. Copyright Office. Cost: $65 online. This gives you statutory damages ($750–$30,000 per infringement, up to $150,000 if willful) instead of having to prove actual damages.

### 2. License Agreement Template

Use a standard software license agreement. Key terms:

- **Grant:** Non-exclusive, non-transferable license to use specified IP
- **Scope:** Define exactly which components are licensed (list them)
- **Territory:** Worldwide unless restricted
- **Term:** Perpetual or annual renewal
- **Restrictions:** No sublicensing, no derivative works without written consent, no removal of attribution
- **Audit rights:** Right to verify compliance annually
- **IP ownership:** All IP remains with Celaya Solutions; licensee gets usage rights only
- **Indemnification:** Licensee indemnifies you against claims arising from their use

### 3. Provisional Patents

File provisionals on:
- **Proof of Coherence** consensus mechanism (Celaya Chain Protocol)
- **OpenClaw** multi-agent robotic control architecture
- **Instrument schema** format (typed input/output/failure boundary specification for research tools)
- **Cognitive load delta** timing system (Volt instrument)

Cost: $320 each ($1,280 total). Buys 12 months to evaluate commercial viability before full filing ($2,000–$5,000 each with a patent attorney).

### 4. Notarization / Prior Art

Run the EPPE notarization on:
- `CODEBASE_VALUATION_REPORT.txt` (already hashed)
- `data/instruments.ts` (the instrument schema)
- `AGENTS.md` (the 37-agent taxonomy)
- `components/ResearchEcosystem.tsx` (the 12-system inventory)

On-chain timestamps via Solana memo program create immutable proof of existence at a specific date. Cost: ~$0.01 per notarization.

3. **Risk cost** — Shipping without governance frameworks and owning the liability

5. **Opportunity cost** — Every month they spend building infrastructure is a month they are not building product

1. The valuation report proves the work is real and quantified
2. The on-chain hash proves the work existed before they started
3. The typed schemas and governance docs prove this is not a prototype
4. The 37-agent naming convention proves depth of thought most teams never reach

### The Line That Closes

> "The license costs less than one month of the senior engineer you would need to hire to start rebuilding this from scratch. And that engineer would not have it done for five months."

## What Not to Do

1. **Do not open-source the core schemas.** Open-source the React components if you want visibility. Keep the instrument taxonomy, agent naming convention, governance frameworks, and system architectures behind the license wall.

2. **Do not negotiate against yourself.** State the price. Attach the valuation report. Let them do the math.

3. **Do not sell to everyone.** Exclusivity in a vertical (e.g., "exclusive license for civic tech in Texas") commands 3–5x premium over non-exclusive.

4. **Do not license without a signed agreement.** Even for friends. Especially for friends.

5. **Do not undervalue the governance IP.** The ISO alignment mapping and behavioral standards are unsexy but they are what enterprise compliance teams will pay real money for. Lead with them when talking to large companies.
