Technical Specification
MERIDIAN.
In detail.
5
Active Domains
Electrical infrastructure, AI architecture, audio production, civic accountability, cognitive science. MERIDIAN formally maps the pattern recognition that happens naturally across all five.
Meta
Operating Layer
MERIDIAN sits above CLOS and all other instruments. It is not one of the 37 CLOS agents. It is the framework that connects the instruments to each other.
Cross-Domain
Pattern Recognition
The core function: identifying structural similarities across domains that would not be visible if you stayed inside any single domain. Electrical system architecture and AI agent coordination share patterns. MERIDIAN makes those explicit.
Formal
Not Intuitive
Christopher has always operated across domain boundaries intuitively. MERIDIAN is the project of making that intuition formal, documented, and transmissible.
CLOS
Integration
MERIDIAN is embedded in the CLOS architecture as the meta-cognitive agent. It receives pattern reports from all 37 CLOS agents and identifies cross-domain coherence signals.
Research
Ongoing
MERIDIAN is an active research framework. The domain map is being refined. New cross-domain patterns are being documented as they emerge from the research ecosystem.
The patterns others miss are only invisible
if you stay inside one domain.

MERIDIAN emerged from a simple observation: Christopher consistently identified patterns across domains that specialists in each domain had not connected. The meta-framework is the formalization of that capability.
