Technical Specification
SIGNAL.
In detail.
172k+
Audio Sample Library
Over 172,000 audio samples as a research corpus. Not a production sample library — a psychoacoustic research database. Patterns, frequencies, emotional triggers, and their correlations are indexed.
Frisson
Analyzer Architecture
The frisson analyzer detects psychoacoustic triggers for involuntary emotional responses. Local processing only. No API cost. The analysis runs on device.
Local
Processing
All signal processing runs locally. The psychoacoustic analysis pipeline does not require internet access. Research data stays in the research environment.
MCP
REAPER Integration
A REAPER DAW MCP server concept that allows the DAW to be driven by AI agents. Audio production as a programmable research instrument.
Research
Stage
SIGNAL is an active research investigation. The relationship between audio patterns, cognitive states, and biometric responses is the open question.
CLOS
Integration
SIGNAL feeds into the CLOS architecture via the Frisson agent. Psychoacoustic triggers that produce measurable biometric responses are logged and analyzed.

Build History
The record.
Library
172,000+ samples.
The audio sample library grew organically through production work. At some point it became large enough to be a research corpus rather than just a workflow resource.
Frisson
The phenomenon named.
Frisson: the involuntary physiological response to audio. Chills, goosebumps, elevated heart rate. SIGNAL is designed to detect the psychoacoustic patterns that reliably trigger it.
MCP
DAW integration.
The REAPER MCP server concept: programming audio production workflows through AI agents. The DAW becomes a research instrument that can be scripted and analyzed.
Now
Active investigation.
SIGNAL is running. The relationship between audio patterns, cognitive states (monitored by CLOS), and biometric responses (monitored by MORTEM) is the open research question.
